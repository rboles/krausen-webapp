Let's Make a Beer

Yeast Starter

Original gravity: {{wortOG.toFixed(3)}} ({{calcPlato(wortOG)}} degrees Plato)

  • Starter volume: {{calcStarterVolume(wortOG, hasStirPlate)}} liters
  • Starter DME: {{calcStarterDme(wortOG, hasStirPlate)}} grams
  1. Add {{calcStarterDme(wortOG, hasStirPlate)}} grams DME and a pinch of yeast nutrient to boiling vessel
  2. Fill vessel to {{calcStarterVolume(wortOG, hasStirPlate)}} liters and boil gently for 15 minutes
  3. Let cool to 70 dF and pitch yeast
  4. Add stir bar, cover flask opening with clean tin foil and aerate on stir plate for 18 to 24 hours
  5. When the starter is ready, put it in the fridge for 24 hours to let the yeast rest and settle.
  6. Pour off extra liquid above yeast cake before pitching.

Brew Water

Pounds of grain: {{poundsGrain}}

Mash temperature: {{mashTemperature}}

  • Strike water ({{mashTemperature + 10}} dF): {{calcStrikeWaterVolume(poundsGrain)}} gallons
  • Pre-lauter (180 dF): {{calcPreLauterVolume(boilVolume, poundsGrain, waterAbsorptionRatio)}} gallons
  • Sparge water (180 dF): {{calcSpargeWaterVolume(boilVolume)}} gallons
  1. Heat {{calcStrikeWaterVolume(poundsGrain)}} gallons of water to ({{mashTemperature + 10}} dF. Pour into mash tun.
  2. Add grain, stir and cover. Mash for 60 to 90 minutes.
  3. Heat {{calcPreLauterVolume(boilVolume, poundsGrain, waterAbsorptionRatio)}} gallons water to 180 dF. Add to mash, stir, wait 20 minutes, vorlauf, drain.
  4. Heat {{calcSpargeWaterVolume(boilVolume)}} gallons water to 180 dF. Add to mash, stir, wait 20 minutes, vorlauf, drain.
  5. Boil kettle should contain {{boilVolume}} gallon liquor.
